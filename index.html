    Welcome to the Trustworthy Knowledge-Driven AI (TKAI) Lab

    At the Trustworthy Knowledge Driven AI (TKAI) Lab, we combine research ideas derived from formal methods, linguistics, cognitive science, and machine learning to efficiently build intelligent systems that are trustworthy, ethical, and secure.

    These models should have a few desired properties, such as robustness and interpretability, such that humans can easily understand the generated information. This means designing agents that are expected to learn desirable behavior with minimal supervision and data that are provably stable and generalize to unseen distribution. We believe these objectives can be stably achieved by designing knowledge-guided, neuro-symbolic agents.

    Our Research

    Neuro-Mimetic AI

    Neuro-Mimetic Approaches

    We design brain-inspired computational models that serve as stable, backpropagation-free alternatives to traditional deep learning.

    • Predictive Coding: Self-correcting hierarchical prediction networks that minimize local errors.
    • Local Representation Alignment: Algorithms replacing global gradients with local target representations.
    • Memory-Augmented RNNs: Stable architectures that generalize to longer sequences with finite precision.

    Key Publications

    • Predictive Coding Survey (2025)
    • PC Stability & Convergence (2024)
    • Backpropagation-Free LRA (2023)
    AI for Science

    AI for Scientific Discovery

    We leverage machine learning to accelerate discoveries and solve complex optimization challenges in physical and biological systems.

    • Enzyme-Substrate Affinity: Using protein language models and graph neural networks to predict kinetics (Kcat).
    • Physics-Informed Neural Networks: Design of predictive-coding-based PINNs to capture high-frequency oscillations.

    Key Publications

    • Enzyme Kinetics with RealKcat (2025)
    Neuro-Symbolic LLMs

    Reasoning & Generalization

    We bridge connectionist language models with symbolic systems to construct interpretable, robust, and provably stable agents.

    • Local RetoMaton: Task-adaptive Weighted Finite Automata (WFA) replacing global search for structured retrieval.
    • LLM Generalization: Minimum Description Length (MDL) and curvature analysis to optimize model selection.

    Key Publications

    • Neuro-Symbolic Local RetoMaton (2025)
    • LLM Optimization Curvature (2026)
    • Pedagogical Teacher/Student LLMs (2025)
    Theoretical Foundations

    Theory & Physical Constraints

    We study the mathematical limits of computation and information theory under realistic thermodynamic constraints.

    • Surprisal-Rényi Free Energy (SRFE): A risk-sensitive free energy interpolating forward and reverse KL.
    • Realizable Circuit Complexity: Circuit design constraints modeling heat dissipation and physical space routing.

    Key Publications

    • Surprisal-Rényi Free Energy (2026)
    • Realizable Circuit Complexity (2025)
    Memory-Augmented Neural Networks

    Memory-Augmented Networks

    We develop memory-augmented recurrent architectures that generalize to sequence lengths far beyond training limits without losing computational efficiency.

    • Neural Turing Machines: Provably stable models computing with finite-precision memory over time.
    • Sequence Generalization: Recurrent models outperforming state-of-the-art Transformers on formal language tasks.

    Key Publications

    • Neural Turing Machine Stability (2024)
    • Exploring Learnability in MANNs (2024)
    • A Neural State Pushdown Automata (2021)
